Devil May Cry

Hideki Kamiya's first action game, one that would change all the rules.

The game began its development as the earliest version of Resident Evil 4, but Kamiya gradually convinced the team to turn it into a separate project.

A lightning-quick and challenging action game that pushed style and player self-expression to the forefront.

  • Studio: Little Devils
  • Publisher: Capcom
  • Director: Hideki Kamiya
  • Year: 2001

Fun Facts

  1. The "juggling" mechanics in the game (keeping enemies airborne through repeated attacks) were intentionally designed after a bug in earlier Capcom action game Onimusha.
  2. Despite the game's tremendous financial and critical success, Kamiya and Team Little Devils were not brought on to develop the ill-fated Devil May Cry 2.
  3. Kamiya would not be directly involved in any future games in the series, but did later direct a spiritual successor, Bayonetta.
